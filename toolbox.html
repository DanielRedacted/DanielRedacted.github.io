Privacy Legislation


United States of America

Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)

  • Full Text
  • Website: https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/rules/childrens-online-privacy-protection-rule-coppa

  • Effective: 2000
  • Scope: Governs the collection of personal information from children under 13 by websites or online services.
  • Key Provisions:
    • Parental consent requirement
    • Data collection limitations
    • Privacy policy transparency

California

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

  • Full Text
  • https://www.oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa

  • Effective: 2020
  • Scope: Applies to businesses collecting personal data from California residents.
  • Key Provisions:
    • Right to know
    • Right to delete
    • Right to opt-out of sale or sharing
    • Right to correct
    • Right to limit use and disclosure of sensitive personal information
    • Right to non-discrimination
  • Financial penalties for violations.

California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)

  • Full Text
  • https://privacy.ca.gov/

  • Effective: 2023
  • Scope: An amendment to CCPA, enhancing protections and creating the California Privacy Protection Agency.
  • Key Provisions: Expands consumer rights, requires more transparency, and strengthens enforcement.

California Delete Act (SB 362)

  • Full Text

  • Effective: January 1, 2026
  • Scope: The law requires data brokers to delete consumers' personal data upon request and creates a centralized system to simplify data deletion.
  • Key Provisions:
    • Creation of a "Delete Button"
    • Data Broker Registry
    • Mandatory Data Deletion
    • Penalties for Non-Compliance

Texas

Texas Privacy Protection Act (HB 3746)

  • Full Text

  • Effective: September 2021
  • Scope: Requires businesses to notify the Texas Attorney General when they experience data breaches that affect 250 or more Texas residents.
  • Key Provisions:
    • Affected businesses must submit breach reports and notify the individuals affected by the breach.
    • The Texas Attorney General publishes this information, creating transparency regarding which businesses have suffered breaches.

Texas Business and Commerce Code - Chapter 521: Identity Theft Enforcement and Protection Act

  • Full Text

  • Effective: 2005
  • Scope: Regulates the collection, use, and disposal of personal information in Texas.
  • Key Provisions:
    • Requires businesses to implement reasonable procedures to protect personal information.
    • Mandates notification to Texas residents if their sensitive personal information is breached.
    • Defines penalties for failing to protect personal data.

Capture or Use of Biometric Identifier Act (CUBI Act)

  • Full Text
  • Website: https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/consumer-protection/file-consumer-complaint/consumer-privacy-rights/biometric-identifier-act

  • Effective: 2009
  • Scope: Regulates the collection and use of biometric identifiers such as fingerprints, retina scans, or facial recognition data.
  • Key Provisions:
    • Requires businesses to obtain consent before collecting biometric data.
    • Prohibits the sale or disclosure of biometric identifiers unless certain conditions are met.
    • Imposes penalties for improper use or storage of biometric data.

European Union (EU)

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

  • Full Text
  • Website: https://gdpr.eu

  • Effective: 2018
  • Scope: Applies to all companies processing the personal data of individuals residing in the EU, regardless of the company’s location.
  • Key Provisions: Requires consent for data processing, grants data subject rights (access, correction, deletion), mandates data breach notifications, and imposes severe fines for non-compliance.

Switzerland

Art. 13 Swiss Constitution (Right to Privacy)

  • Full Text

  • Effective: April 18, 1999
  • Scope: Guarantees the right to personal privacy, including the protection of personal data and respect for private and family life. This article is part of the Swiss Federal Constitution and forms the constitutional foundation for privacy rights in Switzerland.
  • Key Provisions:
    • Right to Privacy: Every person has the right to privacy in their private and family life, in their home, and in their correspondence and telecommunications.
    • Protection of Personal Data: Every person has the right to be protected against the misuse of their personal data.

Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP)

  • Full Text

  • Effective: 1992 (Updated September 1, 2023)
  • Scope: Applies to both public and private sectors that process personal data of individuals in Switzerland.
  • Key Provisions:
    • Data Protection Principles: Requires organizations to process personal data in a lawful, transparent, and secure manner. Data must be collected for specific, legitimate purposes, and kept no longer than necessary.
    • Consent Requirement: Organizations must obtain explicit consent from individuals before processing sensitive personal data (e.g., health, religious beliefs, political opinions).
    • Cross-Border Data Transfers: Personal data can only be transferred to countries with adequate data protection laws, or when appropriate safeguards (like contracts or consent) are in place.
    • Data Breach Notifications: Companies must notify the Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC) and affected individuals if there is a breach of personal data security.
    • Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs): Required for processing activities that pose high risks to individuals’ privacy, ensuring compliance with privacy principles.
    • Data Subject Rights:
      • Right to access: Individuals can request information about their personal data being processed.
      • Right to correction: Individuals can ask for their inaccurate data to be corrected.
      • Right to deletion: Individuals can request the deletion of their data if there is no legitimate reason for its continued retention.
